MATHS FOR CODING (Standard SUS Formula Reference)
* Odd questions (1, 3, 5, 7, 9) contribution = score - 1
* Even questions (2, 4, 6, 8, 10) contribution = 5 - score
* Total SUS Score = sum of all contributions * 2.5 (scales to 0-100)


DESIGN REFLECTIONS & VIVA NOTES
Just some notes to remind myself why we're using this exact layout for my final report/viva questions:

1. Why the Alternating Odd/Even Question Polarity?
   - Odd questions are positive, even questions are negative. This is a deliberate psychometric control called "acquiescence bias mitigation".
   - If all questions were positive, tired evaluators (especially contract recruiters!) might just click "Strongly Agree" (5) all the way down without reading. 
   - By alternating the polarity, participants are forced to actually read each sentence, otherwise they end up entering self-contradictory ratings. It keeps the data clean and honest.

2. Why the 1-5 Likert Scale?
   - The 5-point scale is the industry sweet spot.
   - 3-point scales (disagree, neutral, agree) are too narrow and hide nuances in trust.
   - 7-point or 10-point scales add cognitive fatigue—evaluators waste time debating if their frustration is a 7 or an 8.
   - A 5-point scale gives a clear, neutral center point (3) while making it quick and easy to express strong feelings (1 or 5) without over-analyzing.
   - Crucially, this is the exact scale validated by John Brooke (1986) to guarantee standard usability benchmarking.
