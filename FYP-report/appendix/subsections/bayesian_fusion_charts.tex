\subsection*{Step-by-Step Walkthrough}
\label{appendix:bayesian-fusion-walkthrough}

\textbf{Step 1: CV assertion.}\ We start from a sparse prior ($\alpha_0 = \beta_0 = 0.1$, score $0.500$), with repeated CV 
mentions leading to a strong signal ($s_1 = 0.8$, $c_1 = 0.5$), lifting the score to $0.711$ ($\alpha = 0.505$, $\beta = 0.205$, 
$\sigma$: $0.456 \rightarrow 0.347$). On CV text alone, the candidate looks like a strong match, and this would usually be 
the outcome of a CV-only screening pipeline.

\textbf{Step 2: GitHub contradiction.}\ Zero GitHub signal ($s_2 = 0.0$, $c_2 = 0.9$) pulls the score to $0.314$ 
($\beta = 1.106$). A confident verifiable contradiction overrides the CV claim. Proprietary work without public 
repos records uncertainty rather than a hard rejection. Alternatively, could be a candidate who has demonstrated evidence
for other skills, but not the one in question.

\textbf{Step 3: LinkedIn endorsement.}\ Claimed LinkedIn ($s_3 = 0.8$, $c_3 = 0.2$) nudges the score to $0.369$ 
only. Self-report cannot replace demonstrated evidence. Given frequent CV embellishment, MERIT intentionally 
favours candidates who can show public work.

\textbf{Demonstration-only profile.}\ For brevity, we skip the demonstration-only profile, we have ($s_1 = 0.0$, $c_1 = 0.5$; $s_2 = 1.0$, $c_2 = 0.9$; $s_3 = 0.0$, $c_3 = 0.2$). 
With no CV mention, the score dips to $0.148$ then rises to $0.624$ after 
GitHub ($s_{\mathrm{GH}} = 1.0$), settling at $0.555$. Despite more self-reported text, the claims-only profile 
finishes 19 points lower ($0.369$). See Table~\ref{tab:bayesian-scenario-comparison} 
and the right column of Figure~\ref{fig:bayesian-fusion-convergence} for the demonstration-only profile.
