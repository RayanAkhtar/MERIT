\section{Threats to Validity}
\label{sec:threats-to-validity}

The evaluation presented in this chapter provides a broad and multi-faceted assessment of the MERIT architecture. 
However, no empirical study is without methodological limitations, and it is essential to identify the factors 
that may constrain the generalisability and strength of the conclusions drawn. Following the established taxonomy of 
Wohlin et al.~\cite{wohlin_threats}, we organise these threats into four categories: \textit{construct validity}, 
\textit{internal validity}, \textit{external validity}, and \textit{conclusion validity}. Through a total of twelve distinct 
evaluation studies, we have attempted to provide convergent evidence of systemic robustness while maintaining transparency 
regarding these limitations.
