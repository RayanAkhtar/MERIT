\chapter{Evaluation}
\label{ch:evaluation}

This chapter presents the results of the rigorous mathematical, computational, and human trials conducted
to validate the MERIT architecture. The evaluation suite is designed to test the system's functionality
and validate its three core pillars: Extensibility, Multi-Source Fusion, and Explainability.

\subsection*{Codebase Traceability \& Evaluation Mapping}
To ensure transparency and auditability, our evaluation structure maintains a one-to-one mapping with the
active implementation files and subdirectories. This is located within the github repository's \textit{/evaluation/}
directory. In this chapter, we will refer to a distinct study with a numerical prefix. This prefix correlates directly
to the prefix of the study within the evaluation directory. For example, Study 04 (Information Retrieval
Parser Accuracy) corresponds to the \texttt{04-ir\_parser\_test/} directory.

Rather than presenting the studies in numerical order, we will instead present them in the logical order of the
MERIT pipeline. This allows us to build up the results section in a logical, bottom-up manner. We will first test MERIT's
ingestion, extensibility, and dynamic metric extraction foundation, followed by its core scoring and multi-source fusion
engine (comprising temporal decay, source and algorithmic ablations, and conflict resolution). Finally, we test the
explainability layer through game-theoretic checks and recruiter trials. We then evaluate human-machine expert alignment
through Spearman's correlation, conclude with fairness and computational rigour, and discuss threats to validity. This ensures
that the reader can follow the evaluation in a logical order, building up a comprehensive understanding of the system.

\textbf{Figures.} Numeric results are presented in the main chapter as tables where possible. 
Bar charts, pie charts, and other large figures are deferred to the appendix and cited inline.
