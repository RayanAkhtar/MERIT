\chapter{Evaluation}
\label{ch:evaluation}

This chapter presents the results of the rigorous mathematical, computational, and human trials conducted
to validate the MERIT architecture. First, we will evaluate whether the three pillars of MERIT are working as intended.
From there, we continue with a series of human trials to evaluate its usability in real-world scenarios. From there,
we then test the system's fairness and computational scalability, both spacetime and runtime. Finally, we conclude with
a discussion of the many threats to validity towards both the generation and interpretation of the results throughout
this chapter.

\subsection*{Codebase Traceability \& Evaluation Mapping}
To ensure transparency and auditability, our evaluation structure maintains a one-to-one mapping with the
active implementation files and subdirectories. This is located within the project repository's \textit{evaluation/}
directory~\cite{merit_repo}. In this chapter, we will refer to a distinct study with a numerical prefix. This prefix correlates directly
to the prefix of the study within the evaluation directory. For example, Study 04 would be found in the \texttt{04-ir\_parser\_test/} directory
due to the $04$ prefix.


Note that while the numbering of the studies is in terms of their implementation, we alter the order in which
we present these studies in order to display the results and findings in a logical, bottom-up manner that is
easier to follow. 

Numeric results are presented in the main chapter as tables where possible. 
Bar charts, pie charts, and other large figures are deferred to the appendix and cited inline.
